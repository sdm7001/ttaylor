## 1. Uncontested Divorce (No Children, No Property)

### Trigger Conditions
- Client requests divorce
- Parties have no minor children together (biological or adopted)
- No real property, significant community property, or community debt to divide
- Both parties agree to the divorce and its terms
- Neither party is in the military on active duty (or military provisions are addressed)

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Capture client info, run conflict check against all firm records, verify no children or property | Paralegal |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate Original Petition for Divorce; verify residency requirements (6 months Texas, 90 days Harris County) | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File Original Petition via eFileTexas; obtain cause number from Odyssey | Paralegal |
| **Service / Waiver** | Obtain Waiver of Service from respondent (preferred) or arrange service of process | Paralegal |
| **60-Day Waiting Period** | Texas mandatory cooling-off period (TFC § 6.702); system tracks deadline automatically | System |
| **Final Documents Drafting** | Generate Final Decree of Divorce, Vital Statistics form (VS-165), and any ancillary documents | Paralegal |
| **Attorney Review** | Attorney reviews and approves all final documents | Attorney (approval gate) |
| **Respondent Approval** | Respondent (or respondent's attorney) approves final decree as to form and substance | Paralegal |
| **Prove-Up Hearing / Submission** | Submit agreed decree to court; some Harris County courts allow submission without hearing for fully uncontested cases | Attorney |
| **Final Order Entry** | Court signs Final Decree of Divorce; file conformed copy; update matter status | Paralegal |
| **Post-Decree Tasks** | File certified copies with county clerk if name change; distribute copies to client; close matter | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (client)
- Respondent (opposing spouse)

### Critical Documents
- Original Petition for Divorce
- Waiver of Service (or Citation + Return of Service)
- Financial Information Statement (if required by court)
- Final Decree of Divorce
- Vital Statistics Form (VS-165)
- Employer Order (if spousal maintenance applies, rare in this matter type)

### Filing Requirements
- All documents e-filed through eFileTexas
- Original Petition filed in Harris County district court or county court at law
- Filing fee paid at time of petition filing
- Final Decree submitted as filing packet with lead document + any attachments

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Petition approval** — Attorney reviews petition before filing
2. **Final decree approval** — Attorney reviews and approves final decree before submission to court

### Typical Timeline
- Minimum 60 days (statutory waiting period)
- Typical: 60-90 days if waiver obtained promptly and court calendar allows

### Common Edge Cases
- Respondent refuses to sign waiver → must arrange formal service, may convert to contested
- Respondent is in the military → must comply with Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA); may need military affidavit and additional waiting period
- Respondent cannot be located → must pursue service by publication (TRC Rule 109), significantly extends timeline
- Discovery of undisclosed property during process → may need to reclassify as uncontested with property or contested
- Party files bankruptcy during pendency → automatic stay may affect property division aspects
- Residency requirements not met → must wait or refile in correct jurisdiction

## 2. Uncontested Divorce (With Children and Property)

### Trigger Conditions
- Client requests divorce
- Parties have minor children together and/or community property/debt to divide
- Both parties agree on all terms: conservatorship, possession, child support, property division
- Agreement reached through negotiation or mediation (not contested hearing)

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Capture client info, identify children and property, run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Information Gathering** | Collect financial records, property inventories, employment info, child-related information (school, medical, activities) | Paralegal |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate Original Petition for Divorce with prayer for conservatorship, child support, and property division | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File Original Petition; obtain cause number | Paralegal |
| **Standing Order Activation** | Harris County Standing Order takes effect automatically upon filing (petitioner) and service (respondent) | System (tracking) |
| **Service / Waiver** | Obtain Waiver of Service or arrange service of process | Paralegal |
| **Negotiation / MSA** | If not already agreed: facilitate negotiation of terms; if mediation needed, schedule and track | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Child Support Calculation** | Run child support guidelines worksheet based on obligor's net resources | Paralegal |
| **Final Documents Drafting** | Generate Final Decree of Divorce including: conservatorship provisions, possession order (SPO or modified), child support and medical support provisions, property division, QDRO if retirement accounts involved | Paralegal |
| **Income Withholding Order** | Generate Income Withholding Order for child support | Paralegal |
| **Attorney Review** | Attorney reviews all final documents for legal sufficiency | Attorney (approval gate) |
| **Respondent Approval** | Respondent approves final decree as to form and substance | Paralegal |
| **Prove-Up Hearing** | Attend prove-up hearing; present testimony establishing jurisdictional facts and best interest of children | Attorney |
| **Final Order Entry** | Court signs decree and IWO; file conformed copies | Paralegal |
| **Post-Decree Implementation** | File IWO with employer; prepare and submit QDRO (if applicable); transfer property deeds; update insurance beneficiaries; file name change documents; distribute certified copies | Paralegal |
| **Matter Close** | Verify all post-decree tasks complete; close matter | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (client)
- Respondent (opposing spouse)
- Children (referenced in pleadings; may have Ad Litem if court appoints one)
- Amicus Attorney or Guardian ad Litem (if appointed by court)

### Critical Documents
- Original Petition for Divorce
- Waiver of Service or Citation + Return of Service
- Financial Information Statement
- Child Support Worksheet
- Mediated Settlement Agreement (if mediated)
- Final Decree of Divorce
- Possession Order (Standard or Modified)
- Income Withholding Order
- Medical Support Order
- QDRO (if retirement plan division)
- Special Warranty Deed or Deed of Trust to Secure Assumption (if real property transfer)
- Vital Statistics Form (VS-165)

### Filing Requirements
- All documents e-filed through eFileTexas
- Income Withholding Order filed separately from decree (different filing code)
- QDRO filed as separate cause or under same cause number depending on court practice
- Property transfer deeds recorded with Harris County Clerk (separate from court filing)

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Petition approval** — Before filing
2. **MSA review** — Before client signs mediated settlement agreement
3. **Final decree and all ancillary orders approval** — Before submission to court
4. **QDRO approval** — Before submission to plan administrator and court

### Typical Timeline
- Minimum 60 days (statutory waiting period)
- Typical: 90-180 days depending on complexity of property division and negotiation timeline
- QDRO processing may extend 30-60 days post-decree

### Common Edge Cases
- One party's income is unclear or disputed → may need discovery or forensic accounting even in "uncontested" case
- Complex property (business interests, stock options, multiple real properties) → may require appraisals and expert valuation
- Retirement plan administrator rejects QDRO → must revise and resubmit; track carefully
- Party relocates during pendency → may affect geographic restriction in possession order
- Child turns 18 during pendency → child support and conservatorship provisions may need adjustment
- Undisclosed debt discovered → property division must be revised
- Existing protective order → must be referenced in and consistent with final decree

## 3. Contested Divorce

### Trigger Conditions
- Client requests divorce
- Parties cannot agree on one or more material terms (property division, conservatorship, possession, support)
- Respondent files counter-petition or contests petition terms
- High-conflict situation requiring litigation

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Enhanced intake: assess conflict level, identify safety concerns, evaluate complexity | Paralegal |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter with appropriate retainer (higher for contested); discuss fee structure | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Petition Drafting & Filing** | Generate and file Original Petition for Divorce; may include request for temporary orders | Paralegal |
| **Service of Process** | Arrange formal service via constable, sheriff, or private process server | Paralegal |
| **Temporary Orders** | File Motion for Temporary Orders; prepare for temporary orders hearing; draft proposed temporary orders | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Temporary Orders Hearing** | Hearing before associate judge; testimony and evidence; court enters temporary orders | Attorney |
| **Discovery** | Propound and respond to discovery: Interrogatories, RFPs, RFAs; issue subpoenas for third-party records; schedule depositions | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Expert Engagement** | Retain experts as needed: appraisers, forensic accountants, custody evaluators, vocational experts | Attorney |
| **Social Study / Custody Evaluation** | If children involved: request DRO social study or private custody evaluation | Paralegal |
| **Mediation** | Court-ordered or voluntary mediation; prepare mediation statement; attend mediation | Attorney + Paralegal |
| **Trial Preparation** | If mediation fails: prepare trial notebooks, witness lists, exhibit lists, proposed findings of fact, proposed orders | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Pre-Trial Conference** | Attend pre-trial conference; submit pre-trial order | Attorney |
| **Trial** | Present evidence, examine witnesses, argue to court | Attorney |
| **Post-Trial** | Draft final orders consistent with court's ruling; submit to court for signature | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Post-Decree Implementation** | Same as uncontested divorce with property/children, plus potential appeal considerations | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (client)
- Respondent (opposing spouse)
- Respondent's attorney
- Children (if any; may have court-appointed attorney)
- Amicus Attorney / Guardian ad Litem / Attorney Ad Litem (if appointed)
- Expert witnesses (appraisers, forensic accountants, custody evaluators)

### Critical Documents
- Original Petition for Divorce (and Counter-Petition from respondent)
- Motion for Temporary Orders + proposed Temporary Orders
- Discovery requests and responses (Interrogatories, RFPs, RFAs)
- Subpoenas (duces tecum and/or ad testificandum)
- Mediation Statement (confidential)
- Mediated Settlement Agreement (if mediation successful)
- Trial exhibits, witness list, exhibit list
- Proposed Findings of Fact and Conclusions of Law
- Final Decree of Divorce
- All ancillary orders (IWO, medical support, QDRO, property transfer deeds)

### Filing Requirements
- All documents e-filed through eFileTexas
- Discovery exchanged between parties (not filed with court except on motion)
- Trial exhibits pre-marked and exchanged per local rules
- Proposed orders submitted to court after trial per judge's instructions

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Petition approval** — Before filing
2. **Temporary orders hearing preparation** — Attorney directs strategy
3. **Discovery responses** — Attorney reviews before serving
4. **Mediation authority** — Attorney confirms client's settlement authority before mediation
5. **Trial strategy and preparation** — Attorney directs all trial preparation
6. **Final orders** — Attorney reviews before submission

### Typical Timeline
- Minimum 60 days (statutory waiting period)
- Typical: 6-18 months depending on complexity and court docket
- Complex property or high-conflict custody: may exceed 18 months

### Common Edge Cases
- Family violence → may need immediate protective order; affects temporary orders and conservatorship
- Party hides assets → forensic accounting; possible sanctions for discovery abuse
- Child's preference testimony → child 12+ may express preference to judge in chambers (TFC § 153.009)
- Relocation during pendency → emergency motion to prevent or allow relocation
- Substance abuse allegations → court-ordered drug testing through DRO
- CPS involvement → must coordinate with CPS case; may affect conservatorship determination
- Military deployment → SCRA protections; stay of proceedings; modified possession provisions
- Business valuation disputes → dueling experts; court must choose valuation methodology
- De novo appeal of associate judge's temporary orders → 30-day deadline; brief to referring judge

## 4. SAPCR — Original

### Trigger Conditions
- Parent, Attorney General, or other person with standing seeks to establish conservatorship, possession, and/or child support for a child
- No existing court order regarding the child (or existing order is from another state requiring Texas registration)
- Parents were never married, or child's parentage needs establishment
- Grandparent, relative, or non-parent caregiver seeks custody

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Identify all parties with potential interest in the child; run comprehensive conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Standing Analysis** | Verify client has standing to file under TFC § 102.003-102.006 | Attorney |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate Original Petition in SAPCR; include requests for conservatorship, possession, child support, medical support | Paralegal |
| **Paternity (if applicable)** | If parentage not established: include paternity allegations; may need genetic testing order | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Filing** | e-File Original Petition in appropriate Harris County court | Paralegal |
| **Service of Process** | Serve all required parties (both parents, any alleged father, any person with possession of child) | Paralegal |
| **Temporary Orders** | File motion for temporary orders if needed; prepare for hearing | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Discovery** | Propound and respond to discovery as needed | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Social Study / Evaluation** | Request DRO social study or private evaluation if custody disputed | Paralegal |
| **Mediation** | Attempt mediation; prepare mediation statement | Attorney + Paralegal |
| **Final Orders Preparation** | Draft final orders; calculate child support; prepare possession schedule | Paralegal |
| **Attorney Review** | Attorney reviews all final documents | Attorney (approval gate) |
| **Hearing / Trial** | Prove-up (if agreed) or trial (if contested) | Attorney |
| **Order Entry** | Court signs final order; file conformed copies | Paralegal |
| **Post-Order Implementation** | File IWO with employer; set up payment through SDU; distribute certified copies | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (person filing suit)
- Respondent(s) (all persons required to be served under TFC § 102.009)
- Child (referenced in pleadings)
- Intervenors (if any: grandparents, relatives, DFPS)
- Court-appointed representatives (Amicus, Ad Litem, Guardian ad Litem)

### Critical Documents
- Original Petition in SAPCR
- Citation + Return of Service (for each respondent)
- Motion for Temporary Orders + Temporary Orders (if applicable)
- Financial Information Statement
- Child Support Worksheet
- Social Study Report (from DRO or private evaluator)
- Mediated Settlement Agreement (if mediated)
- Final Order in SAPCR
- Standard Possession Order or Modified Possession Order
- Income Withholding Order
- Medical Support Order

### Filing Requirements
- Filed in Harris County district court
- Must join all required parties under TFC § 102.009
- If paternity at issue: comply with TFC Chapter 160 (Uniform Parentage Act)
- Filing fee paid at petition filing

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Standing determination** — Attorney confirms client has legal standing before filing
2. **Petition approval** — Before filing
3. **Final orders** — Before submission to court

### Typical Timeline
- No statutory waiting period (unlike divorce)
- Uncontested: 60-120 days
- Contested: 6-18 months

### Common Edge Cases
- Unknown father → must attempt to identify and serve; may need service by publication
- Interstate jurisdiction (UCCJEA) → must establish Texas as home state; may need to register foreign order
- CPS involvement → DFPS may have its own SAPCR filed; must coordinate or consolidate
- Child over 12 → may express preference to court (TFC § 153.009)
- Non-parent petitioner → higher burden: must prove parental appointment would significantly impair child
- Tribal membership (ICWA) → must comply with Indian Child Welfare Act; notify tribe
- Military parent → address deployment possession schedule provisions

## 5. SAPCR — Modification

### Trigger Conditions
- Existing court order regarding conservatorship, possession, or child support
- Material and substantial change in circumstances since last order (TFC § 156.101)
- Child is 12+ and wishes to change primary conservator
- At least one year since prior order (for conservatorship changes, unless emergency)
- Three years since last child support order and 20% or $100 difference from guidelines

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Obtain copy of existing order; identify all parties; run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Change Analysis** | Document the material and substantial change in circumstances; gather supporting evidence | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate Petition to Modify Parent-Child Relationship; specify which provisions to modify and grounds | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File under existing cause number in same court that issued prior order | Paralegal |
| **Service of Process** | Serve all parties to the prior order | Paralegal |
| **Temporary Orders** | If emergency or immediate change needed: file motion for temporary orders | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Discovery** | Updated financial information, evidence of changed circumstances | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Mediation** | Attempt mediation before trial | Attorney + Paralegal |
| **Final Documents** | Draft modified order; recalculate child support if applicable | Paralegal |
| **Attorney Review** | Attorney reviews all modified order provisions | Attorney (approval gate) |
| **Hearing / Trial** | Prove-up or contested hearing | Attorney |
| **Modified Order Entry** | Court signs modified order; file conformed copies | Paralegal |
| **Post-Order Implementation** | Update IWO if child support changed; notify schools/medical providers if conservatorship changed | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (party seeking modification)
- Respondent (other party to prior order)
- Any intervenors from prior order who retain rights
- Child (referenced)

### Critical Documents
- Certified copy of prior order being modified
- Petition to Modify Parent-Child Relationship
- Evidence of material and substantial change in circumstances
- Updated Financial Information Statement
- Updated Child Support Worksheet (if support modification)
- Mediated Settlement Agreement (if mediated)
- Modified Order
- Updated Income Withholding Order (if support changed)

### Filing Requirements
- Must be filed under the same cause number as the prior order
- Must be filed in the court that issued the prior order (unless transferred)
- Filing fee paid at petition filing

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Change analysis** — Attorney confirms sufficient legal basis for modification
2. **Petition approval** — Before filing
3. **Modified order approval** — Before submission to court

### Typical Timeline
- Uncontested modification: 60-120 days
- Contested modification: 4-12 months

### Common Edge Cases
- Prior order from another county → must transfer or file in original county
- Prior order from another state → must register under UCCJEA before modifying in Texas
- Relocation request → triggers statutory notice requirements (TFC § 156.103-156.105)
- Emergency modification → must show immediate danger to child's health or safety (TFC § 156.006)
- Multiple prior modifications → court may scrutinize pattern; higher burden to show genuine change
- AG involvement in child support → must join AG as party if support modification affects their order

## 6. Child Support Establishment

### Trigger Conditions
- No existing child support order for the child
- Parentage established or needs establishment
- Custodial parent, Attorney General, or DFPS seeks child support
- Typically filed as part of or alongside a SAPCR

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Identify both parents, verify parentage status, run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Income Investigation** | Gather obligor's income information: pay stubs, tax returns, employment records | Paralegal |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate petition requesting child support establishment; include request for medical support | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File petition (may be part of SAPCR petition or standalone) | Paralegal |
| **Service** | Serve obligor/respondent | Paralegal |
| **Child Support Calculation** | Run guidelines worksheet; calculate net resources, apply percentage | Paralegal |
| **Negotiation** | Attempt agreement on support amount; consider deviation factors | Attorney + Paralegal |
| **Hearing** | Present evidence of income and child's needs to court | Attorney |
| **Order Entry** | Court enters child support order; signs IWO | Paralegal |
| **Post-Order** | File IWO with employer; set up SDU account; provide payment information to parties | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (custodial parent or AG)
- Respondent (obligor parent)
- Child (referenced)

### Critical Documents
- Petition for child support (or SAPCR petition with support request)
- Income documentation (pay stubs, W-2s, tax returns)
- Financial Information Statement
- Child Support Worksheet (guidelines calculation)
- Child Support Order
- Medical Support Order
- Income Withholding Order

### Filing Requirements
- Filed in Harris County court with jurisdiction
- Must establish parentage if not already legally established
- Filing fee applies

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Petition approval** — Before filing
2. **Support calculation review** — Attorney verifies guidelines calculation
3. **Order approval** — Before submission to court

### Typical Timeline
- Uncontested: 45-90 days
- Contested (especially if parentage disputed): 3-9 months

### Common Edge Cases
- Obligor's income unknown or disputed → may need discovery, subpoena employer records, or impute income
- Self-employed obligor → complex income calculation; may need forensic accounting
- Obligor has children from other relationships → "other ordered" children reduce net resources
- Income above statutory cap → court must make specific findings to exceed guidelines
- Obligor incarcerated → reduced ability to pay; court may set minimal support or reserve
- Retroactive support → court may order retroactive support up to 4 years (TFC § 154.009)
- Health insurance availability and cost affects medical support allocation

## 7. Child Support Modification

### Trigger Conditions
- Existing child support order in place
- Material and substantial change in circumstances of the child or a person affected by the order (TFC § 156.401)
- OR it has been 3+ years since the order and the current amount differs by 20% or $100 from the guidelines amount (TFC § 156.401(a-1))
- Change in obligor's income (job loss, promotion, new employment)
- Change in child's needs (medical, educational)
- Change in conservatorship arrangement

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Obtain existing order, verify grounds for modification, run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Income Update** | Gather current income documentation for both parties | Paralegal |
| **Grounds Analysis** | Document the material and substantial change or 3-year/20% threshold | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate Petition to Modify Child Support | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File under existing cause number | Paralegal |
| **Service** | Serve respondent | Paralegal |
| **Updated Calculation** | Run new guidelines worksheet with current income | Paralegal |
| **Negotiation / CSRP** | Attempt agreement; consider AG's Child Support Review Process if AG involved | Attorney + Paralegal |
| **Hearing** | Present evidence of changed circumstances and recalculated support | Attorney |
| **Modified Order Entry** | Court signs modified child support order and updated IWO | Paralegal |
| **Post-Order** | File updated IWO with current employer; update SDU records | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (party seeking modification)
- Respondent (other parent)
- Attorney General (if AG has an open case — must be joined as party)

### Critical Documents
- Certified copy of existing child support order
- Petition to Modify Child Support
- Current income documentation (both parties)
- Updated Financial Information Statement
- Updated Child Support Worksheet
- Evidence of material and substantial change
- Modified Child Support Order
- Updated Income Withholding Order

### Filing Requirements
- Filed under existing cause number
- Filed in court that issued prior order
- AG must be notified/joined if they have an active case

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Grounds analysis** — Attorney confirms legal basis for modification
2. **Petition approval** — Before filing
3. **Modified order approval** — Before submission

### Typical Timeline
- Uncontested: 30-90 days
- Contested: 3-6 months
- CSRP (through AG): 2-4 months

### Common Edge Cases
- AG-initiated review conflicts with private modification → must coordinate proceedings
- Obligor voluntarily underemployed → court may impute income at prior earning capacity
- Multiple modifications in short period → court may scrutinize for harassment or bad faith
- Obligor's new spouse's income → generally not considered, but affects household expenses analysis
- Arrearage accumulation during modification pendency → retroactive credit/adjustment issues
- Interstate enforcement complications (UIFSA) → may need to register foreign order first

## 8. Child Support Enforcement / Contempt

### Trigger Conditions
- Existing child support order
- Obligor has failed to make court-ordered payments
- Arrearage has accumulated
- May include failure to maintain health insurance or pay medical expenses

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Obtain existing order, calculate arrearage, run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Arrearage Calculation** | Document every missed or partial payment; calculate total arrearage with interest (6% per annum under TFC § 157.265) | Paralegal |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Payment Records** | Obtain SDU payment records, bank records, and employer records | Paralegal |
| **Motion Drafting** | Generate Motion for Enforcement / Motion for Contempt; must allege specific violations (date, amount due, amount paid, deficiency) | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File under existing cause number | Paralegal |
| **Service** | Personal service required for contempt actions (cannot use alternative service) | Paralegal |
| **Hearing Preparation** | Prepare evidence of violations; organize payment records chronologically | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Hearing** | Present evidence; court determines contempt and arrearages; court may order jail time (suspended or executed), money judgment, attorney's fees | Attorney |
| **Enforcement Order Entry** | Court signs enforcement order specifying arrearages, payment terms, and consequences for future noncompliance | Paralegal |
| **Post-Order** | File updated IWO if needed; monitor compliance with enforcement order; docket review dates | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Movant (obligee or AG)
- Respondent (obligor)
- Attorney General (if AG has open case)

### Critical Documents
- Certified copy of existing child support order
- Motion for Enforcement / Contempt
- Arrearage calculation spreadsheet
- SDU payment history
- Affidavit of Arrearage
- Enforcement Order / Order of Contempt
- Commitment Order (if jail time ordered)
- Updated Income Withholding Order

### Filing Requirements
- Filed under existing cause number
- Personal service mandatory for contempt proceedings
- Must comply with due process requirements for quasi-criminal contempt proceedings

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Arrearage verification** — Attorney reviews calculation before filing
2. **Motion approval** — Before filing (contempt is quasi-criminal; pleading must be precise)
3. **Enforcement order approval** — Before submission to court

### Typical Timeline
- Filing to hearing: 30-60 days (courts prioritize enforcement)
- Resolution: 1-3 months

### Common Edge Cases
- Obligor claims inability to pay → must prove inability was not willful; "purge" conditions
- Partial payments → must track each payment against each period's obligation
- Multiple enforcement actions → cumulative arrearage tracking required
- Obligor incarcerated → enforcement options limited but arrearage continues to accrue
- SDU records incomplete → may need to subpoena employer records and bank statements
- Constitutional issues → obligor has right to counsel in contempt if incarceration is possible
- Obligor has moved out of state → may need to use UIFSA for interstate enforcement

## 9. Adoption (Step-Parent)

### Trigger Conditions
- Step-parent (spouse of custodial parent) seeks to adopt the child
- The other biological parent's rights must be terminated (voluntarily or involuntarily)
- Step-parent has been married to custodial parent and lived with child for required period

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Identify all parties including absent biological parent; run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Consent / Termination Assessment** | Determine if biological parent will voluntarily relinquish rights or if involuntary termination must be pursued | Attorney |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate Petition for Adoption (and Petition for Termination of Parental Rights if needed) | Paralegal |
| **Background Check** | Obtain criminal background check for step-parent; may need DFPS background check | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File in Harris County district court | Paralegal |
| **Service** | Serve biological parent whose rights are to be terminated (personal service required) | Paralegal |
| **Relinquishment Affidavit** | If voluntary: obtain Affidavit of Voluntary Relinquishment of Parental Rights from biological parent (strict statutory requirements under TFC § 161.103) | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Termination Hearing** | If involuntary: present evidence of statutory grounds for termination (TFC § 161.001) | Attorney |
| **Social Study / Home Study** | Court may order post-placement social study (TFC § 107.0519) | Paralegal |
| **Adoption Hearing** | Present testimony; court evaluates best interest of child | Attorney |
| **Decree of Adoption** | Court signs Decree of Adoption; new birth certificate ordered | Paralegal |
| **Post-Decree** | File for new birth certificate with Texas Vital Statistics; update child's records (school, medical, insurance) | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (step-parent adopting)
- Child's custodial parent (consenting to adoption)
- Biological parent whose rights are being terminated
- Child (if 12+, must consent to adoption under TFC § 162.010)
- DFPS (if involved)

### Critical Documents
- Petition for Step-Parent Adoption
- Petition for Termination of Parental Rights (if separate)
- Affidavit of Voluntary Relinquishment (if voluntary)
- Criminal Background Check
- Social Study / Home Study Report
- Consent of Child (if 12+)
- Decree of Termination of Parental Rights
- Decree of Adoption
- Application for New Birth Certificate

### Filing Requirements
- Filed in Harris County district court
- Confidential filing — adoption records are sealed
- Filing fee applies (may be waived for step-parent adoptions in some courts)

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Termination strategy** — Attorney determines voluntary vs. involuntary path
2. **Relinquishment affidavit review** — Attorney verifies statutory compliance
3. **Petition approval** — Before filing
4. **Decree approval** — Before submission to court

### Typical Timeline
- With voluntary relinquishment: 3-6 months
- With involuntary termination: 6-18 months (contested litigation)

### Common Edge Cases
- Biological parent cannot be located → must conduct diligent search; may need service by publication; may seek termination on abandonment grounds
- Biological parent initially agrees then revokes consent → relinquishment is revocable within certain timeframes; must meet irrevocability requirements
- Child has Native American heritage → ICWA applies; must notify tribe; higher evidentiary standard for termination
- Biological parent is incarcerated → must still be served and have opportunity to participate; incarceration alone insufficient for termination
- Existing child support order → termination of parental rights ends future obligation but not accrued arrearage
- Interstate issues → if biological parent in another state, service and hearing logistics complicated

## 10. Adoption (Non-Relative)

### Trigger Conditions
- Prospective adoptive parents seek to adopt a child who is not a step-child or close relative
- May involve private placement, agency placement, or foster-to-adopt
- Parental rights have been or must be terminated
- DFPS involvement if child is in foster care

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Identify all parties; determine placement type; run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Pre-Placement Requirements** | Verify home study completed by licensed agency; background checks; training requirements | Paralegal |
| **Consent / Relinquishment** | Obtain voluntary relinquishment from biological parents or verify court-ordered termination | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate Petition for Adoption; comply with all statutory requirements including ICWA notice | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File in Harris County district court (confidential filing) | Paralegal |
| **ICWA Compliance** | Determine whether ICWA applies; send required notices to tribes; observe waiting periods | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Post-Placement Supervision** | Licensed agency conducts post-placement visits and files report with court | External (tracked by Paralegal) |
| **Hearing** | Adoption hearing; court evaluates best interest; may interview child | Attorney |
| **Decree of Adoption** | Court signs Decree of Adoption; new birth certificate ordered | Paralegal |
| **Post-Decree** | New birth certificate; update all records; finalize insurance and benefits; notify agency of completion | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner(s) (prospective adoptive parents)
- Biological parent(s) (for relinquishment or termination)
- Licensed placing agency (if agency adoption)
- DFPS (if child is in state custody)
- Child (if 12+, must consent)
- Guardian ad Litem or Attorney ad Litem (typically appointed)
- Indian tribe (if ICWA applies)

### Critical Documents
- Home Study Report (from licensed agency)
- Criminal Background Checks (all household members)
- Affidavit of Voluntary Relinquishment or Termination Order
- Petition for Adoption
- ICWA notices and responses
- Post-Placement Supervision Report
- Consent of Child (if 12+)
- Health, Social, Educational, and Genetic History of Child (TFC § 162.005)
- Decree of Adoption
- Application for New Birth Certificate

### Filing Requirements
- Confidential filing in Harris County district court
- Must comply with TFC Chapter 162 and Chapter 163 (Interstate Compact on the Placement of Children if interstate)
- Agency report must be filed before hearing
- Sealed records

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Placement legality review** — Attorney verifies lawful placement
2. **ICWA compliance** — Attorney confirms all notice requirements met
3. **Petition approval** — Before filing
4. **Decree approval** — Before submission

### Typical Timeline
- Agency adoption: 6-12 months after placement
- Foster-to-adopt: Highly variable (6 months to 2+ years depending on termination proceedings)
- Private adoption: 3-9 months after consent/relinquishment

### Common Edge Cases
- Biological father unknown or denies paternity → must comply with TFC § 161.002 (unknown father provisions); publish notice
- Interstate placement → must comply with Interstate Compact on the Placement of Children (ICPC)
- Failed placement → child returned to agency; adoption petition dismissed
- ICWA jurisdiction challenge → tribe may assert jurisdiction; case transferred to tribal court
- Biological parent challenges termination on appeal → adoption cannot finalize until appeal resolved
- Adoption disruption after decree → extremely rare; requires separate proceeding to set aside

## 11. Grandparents' Rights / Possession

### Trigger Conditions
- Grandparent seeks possession of or access to grandchild
- At least one of the statutory standing requirements met under TFC § 153.432 or § 102.004
- Typically: parent incarcerated, deceased, incompetent, or has had child taken by DFPS
- Grandparent has had "substantial past contact" with child

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Assess standing carefully; identify all persons with legal rights to the child; run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Standing Analysis** | Critical step: verify grandparent meets one of the narrow statutory standing requirements; this is heavily litigated | Attorney |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Evidence Gathering** | Document grandparent's relationship with child; gather evidence of parental unfitness if applicable | Paralegal |
| **Petition Drafting** | Generate Petition for Grandparent Access (TFC § 153.432) or Petition in Intervention in existing SAPCR | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File as new SAPCR or intervention in existing cause | Paralegal |
| **Service** | Serve both parents and any other conservators | Paralegal |
| **Temporary Orders** | Request temporary possession if child is in immediate need | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Mediation** | Attempt resolution through mediation | Attorney + Paralegal |
| **Hearing / Trial** | Must overcome constitutional presumption that fit parent acts in child's best interest (Troxel v. Granville) | Attorney |
| **Order Entry** | Court enters possession order (if successful) | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Petitioner (grandparent)
- Both parents (must be served even if one supports grandparent's petition)
- Current conservators (if different from parents)
- Child (referenced)

### Critical Documents
- Petition for Grandparent Access or Petition in Intervention
- Evidence of standing (death certificate, incarceration records, CPS records, etc.)
- Evidence of substantial past contact
- Evidence overcoming parental presumption (if parent opposes)
- Proposed Possession Order
- Final Order granting or denying access

### Filing Requirements
- Filed in Harris County court
- If intervening in existing SAPCR: filed under existing cause number
- If new action: new cause number

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Standing determination** — Attorney must carefully analyze standing before any filing
2. **Constitutional analysis** — Attorney evaluates ability to overcome Troxel presumption
3. **Petition approval** — Before filing
4. **Order approval** — Before submission

### Typical Timeline
- Uncontested (parent agrees): 60-120 days
- Contested: 6-18 months (standing challenges can add months)

### Common Edge Cases
- Standing challenge by parent → dispositive motion before reaching merits; court may dismiss for lack of standing
- Both parents oppose → must overcome strong constitutional presumption; rarely successful absent CPS involvement
- CPS involvement → may strengthen grandparent's position but adds procedural complexity
- Grandparent already has de facto custody → different standing analysis; may seek conservatorship rather than just access
- Interstate grandchild → UCCJEA home state analysis required
- Adoption by third party → generally terminates grandparent standing unless preserved in adoption order
- Existing SAPCR → grandparent must intervene rather than file new suit

## 12. Mediation (Standalone)

### Trigger Conditions
- Parties to an existing case wish to attempt mediation to resolve some or all issues
- Court orders mediation as prerequisite to trial setting
- Parties voluntarily agree to mediation
- May apply to divorce, SAPCR, modification, or enforcement matters

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Mediation Assessment** | Evaluate which issues are appropriate for mediation; screen for family violence (contraindication for joint mediation) | Attorney |
| **Mediator Selection** | Identify and agree on mediator (private mediator, DRO mediation, or collaborative law mediator) | Attorney + Paralegal |
| **Scheduling** | Coordinate schedules of all parties, attorneys, and mediator; book venue | Paralegal |
| **Document Preparation** | Prepare mediation statement (confidential to mediator); gather all relevant financial documents, proposals, and evidence summaries | Paralegal |
| **Client Preparation** | Attorney prepares client for mediation: discuss settlement ranges, likely outcomes, process expectations | Attorney |
| **Mediation Session** | Attend mediation; negotiate; attempt resolution | Attorney + Client |
| **MSA Drafting** | If agreement reached: draft Mediated Settlement Agreement on-site; all parties and attorneys sign | Attorney |
| **Post-Mediation** | If full agreement: draft final orders conforming to MSA; proceed to prove-up | Paralegal |
| **Post-Mediation (Partial)** | If partial agreement: MSA covers agreed issues; remaining issues proceed to trial | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Post-Mediation (Impasse)** | If no agreement: document impasse; proceed to trial preparation | Paralegal + Attorney |

### Required Parties
- All parties to the underlying case
- Attorneys for all represented parties
- Mediator
- Ad litem / Amicus (if appointed and mediator/court requires attendance)

### Critical Documents
- Mediation Statement (confidential)
- Financial documents and proposals
- Mediated Settlement Agreement (if successful)
- Order referring case to mediation (if court-ordered)
- Written objection to mediation (if applicable, e.g., family violence)

### Filing Requirements
- MSA itself is not filed with the court (it is attached to or incorporated into the final order)
- Order of referral to mediation filed by court
- Final orders conforming to MSA are filed per normal procedures

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Mediation appropriateness** — Attorney screens for contraindications (family violence, power imbalance)
2. **Settlement authority** — Attorney confirms client's settlement parameters before mediation
3. **MSA review** — Attorney reviews MSA terms before client signs (critical — MSA is binding)

### Typical Timeline
- Scheduling: 2-6 weeks after decision to mediate
- Mediation session: 4-8 hours (full day common for complex cases)
- Post-mediation final orders: 30-60 days

### Common Edge Cases
- Family violence → mediation may be inappropriate; if ordered, parties must be in separate rooms (shuttle mediation); victim can object under TFC § 153.0071(f)
- Party appears without authority to settle → mediation may be wasted; require written confirmation of authority
- MSA signed but party later wants to revoke → MSA meeting statutory requirements is irrevocable absent fraud, duress, or coercion
- Partial agreement → must carefully delineate which issues are resolved and which remain contested
- Pro se opposing party → power imbalance concerns; mediator must manage carefully
- Child's preferences not addressed → Amicus or Ad Litem should attend or provide input before MSA is finalized
- Tax implications of property division → attorney must evaluate before client signs MSA

## 13. Post-Order Enforcement and Compliance

### Trigger Conditions
- A party has failed to comply with terms of a final order (not limited to child support — includes property division, possession schedule, insurance requirements, name change provisions, QDRO execution, etc.)
- Need to compel compliance through court action
- Distinguish from child support enforcement (see Matter Type 8) — this covers all other order provisions

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Obtain existing order; identify specific provisions violated; run conflict check | Paralegal |
| **Violation Analysis** | Document each violation: what was ordered, what was done (or not done), when, evidence | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Engagement** | Execute engagement letter, collect retainer, create matter | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Remedy Selection** | Determine appropriate enforcement mechanism: contempt, clarification, specific performance, money judgment | Attorney |
| **Motion Drafting** | Generate appropriate motion (Motion to Enforce, Motion for Contempt, Motion to Clarify, Motion for Turnover) | Paralegal |
| **Filing** | e-File under existing cause number | Paralegal |
| **Service** | Personal service if contempt sought; may use alternative service for non-contempt enforcement | Paralegal |
| **Hearing Preparation** | Organize evidence of violations; prepare testimony | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Hearing** | Present evidence; court determines violations and appropriate remedy | Attorney |
| **Enforcement Order Entry** | Court signs enforcement order with specific compliance deadlines and consequences | Paralegal |
| **Compliance Monitoring** | Track compliance with enforcement order; calendar review deadlines | Paralegal |

### Required Parties
- Movant (party seeking enforcement)
- Respondent (non-compliant party)

### Critical Documents
- Certified copy of order being enforced
- Motion for Enforcement / Contempt / Clarification
- Evidence of non-compliance (documented with dates, specifics)
- Proposed Enforcement Order
- Enforcement Order

### Filing Requirements
- Filed under existing cause number
- Personal service required for contempt proceedings
- Standard service acceptable for non-contempt enforcement

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Remedy selection** — Attorney determines legal strategy
2. **Motion approval** — Before filing
3. **Enforcement order approval** — Before submission

### Typical Timeline
- Filing to hearing: 30-60 days
- Resolution: 1-4 months

### Common Edge Cases
- Order provisions ambiguous → may need clarification before enforcement; cannot hold in contempt for ambiguous provisions
- Property already dissipated → may need money judgment rather than specific performance; consider fraudulent transfer claims
- Respondent claims compliance → factual dispute requiring evidentiary hearing
- Statute of limitations → some enforcement actions have time limits (e.g., property division enforcement under TFC § 9.003: 2 years)
- Respondent in bankruptcy → automatic stay may affect property-related enforcement but not child-related enforcement
- Multiple violations across multiple order provisions → must plead each separately and specifically

## 14. Protective Order / Emergency TRO

### Trigger Conditions
- Victim of family violence, dating violence, sexual assault, stalking, or trafficking seeks court protection
- Imminent danger requiring immediate court intervention
- Applicant is a family member, household member, or person in a dating relationship with the respondent
- May be filed as standalone action or in conjunction with divorce or SAPCR

### Key Workflow Stages

| Stage | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Safety Assessment** | Immediate safety screening; assess lethality risk; identify safe communication channels | Paralegal + Attorney |
| **Intake & Conflict Check** | Expedited intake; capture essential information; run conflict check (prioritize speed with safety) | Paralegal |
| **ACP Enrollment** | If applicable: assist client with Address Confidentiality Program enrollment; suppress address from all records | Paralegal |
| **Application Drafting** | Generate Application for Protective Order; include detailed allegations of violence with dates, descriptions, witnesses | Paralegal |
| **TRO Request** | Draft request for ex parte Temporary Restraining Order; prepare Temporary Ex Parte Protective Order | Paralegal |
| **Emergency Filing** | e-File Application and TRO request immediately (most courts accept same-day emergency filings) | Paralegal |
| **TRO Hearing** | Ex parte hearing (may be same day); judge reviews application and affidavit; enters TRO if immediate danger found | Attorney |
| **Service of TRO** | TRO must be personally served on respondent by law enforcement; coordinates with constable or sheriff | Paralegal |
| **Protective Order Hearing** | Full hearing within 14 days of TRO (extendable to 28 days); both parties present evidence | Attorney |
| **Protective Order Entry** | Court enters protective order (up to 2 years; lifetime in certain cases); order entered into TCIC/NCIC databases | Paralegal |
| **Post-Order Safety Planning** | Provide client with certified copies; advise on enforcement; update safety plan; coordinate with victim services | Paralegal + Attorney |

### Required Parties
- Applicant (victim or person filing on behalf of victim)
- Respondent (alleged abuser)
- Protected persons (may include children and other household members)
- Law enforcement (for service and enforcement)

### Critical Documents
- Application for Protective Order
- Affidavit in Support of Application (sworn statement of violence/threat)
- Request for Temporary Ex Parte Protective Order
- Temporary Restraining Order (TRO)
- Protective Order (final)
- TCIC/NCIC entry form
- Safety Plan documentation

### Filing Requirements
- No filing fee for protective order applications (TFC § 81.002)
- Emergency filing procedures available (many Harris County courts have dedicated PO dockets)
- TRO can be issued ex parte (without notice to respondent)
- Final protective order requires hearing with notice to respondent
- Protective order must be reported to law enforcement databases (TCIC/NCIC)

### Attorney Approval Gates
1. **Application review** — Attorney reviews allegations and legal sufficiency before filing
2. **TRO hearing** — Attorney (or supervised paralegal in some courts) presents TRO request to judge
3. **Protective order hearing preparation** — Attorney directs hearing strategy
4. **Final order review** — Attorney reviews before submission

### Typical Timeline
- TRO: Same day or next business day
- Protective Order hearing: Within 14-28 days of TRO
- Final Protective Order duration: Up to 2 years (can be extended or made permanent)

### Common Edge Cases
- Respondent also files protective order (mutual applications) → court evaluates both; mutual protective orders disfavored but possible
- Children involved → protective order may include temporary conservatorship provisions; coordinate with any pending SAPCR
- Respondent possesses firearms → federal law (18 U.S.C. § 922(g)(8)) prohibits firearm possession under qualifying protective order; include surrender provision
- Respondent is law enforcement → additional sensitivity; may need to file in different jurisdiction; firearm surrender impacts employment
- Victim recants or wants to dismiss → attorney and court evaluate safety; court may maintain order despite victim's request
- Violation of protective order → criminal offense (TFC § 87.001); Class A misdemeanor or third-degree felony for repeat violations; contact law enforcement
- Workplace violence component → may include workplace address in protected locations; coordinate with employer security
- Immigration implications → protective order may support VAWA self-petition or U-visa application
- ACP participant → all filings must use substitute address; court must seal actual address

## Cross-Cutting Workflow Considerations

### Attorney Approval Gates (Universal)
Every matter type includes explicit attorney approval gates. The platform enforces these gates by preventing document submission to eFileTexas or court without a recorded attorney approval action (attorney user, timestamp, and optional note).

### Deadline Management
All matter types generate calculated deadlines based on filing dates, service dates, and court rules. Critical deadlines include:
- Answer deadline (first Monday after 20 days from service)
- Discovery response deadline (30 days from receipt)
- TRO expiration (14 days, extendable to 28)
- 60-day waiting period (divorce)
- Appeal deadlines (varies by order type)
- Statute of limitations for enforcement actions

### Document State Machine
All documents across all matter types follow the same lifecycle: Draft → Generated → Internal Review → Attorney Review → Approved → Signed → Filed. The specific approval requirements and filing packets vary by matter type and document type.

### Conflict Check Integration
Every matter type begins with a conflict check. The conflict check searches across all matter types, all statuses (including closed and declined matters), and all party roles (client, opposing party, child, witness, intervenor).
